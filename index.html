Summary

Lead With A Story is a highly engaging book that proves stories sell. In it Paul Smith shares that involve people in the communication process, which results in the desired outcome of the speaker.

Apart from explaining how to use a story, Smith also explainshow to create or borrow one. Each point is appropriately made with a story, along with a lot of facts and figures spread throughout the book. Stories help the listener to experience and learn from the teller's challenge and/or solutiion.

Structure Of A Story

If you ask a child, What's the order of a good story? they'll respond with, Oh, that's easy there's a beginning, a middle and an end. However if you ask a script writer the same question, they might respond and say there are six part:

the setup, catalyst, first turning point, climax, final confrontation and resolution.

Every answer will differ from person to person, therefore all a leader needs is a simple structure that works. everyone is natural-born storyteller, since we grew up listening to bedtime stories, which helped us to know what's a good structure for a story.

Tips

A useful tip for remembering the basic structure of a story is to use CAR

Context- This provides all the necessary background for the story to make sense. If done right, it generates interest and excitement in the audience, which then makes them listen to the rest of the story.
Action- This is where you tell the ups and downs that happens to the main character. These help build excitement in the story.
Result- This is the final stage where you not only explain the right lesson intended to be learnt by the audience, but also the reason why you told the story in the first place.

Reasons Telling Stories Works

  1. Storytelling is simple. Anyone can do it. You don't need to have a degree in English to tell one.
  2. They're timeless. Unlike fads in other areas of management, storytelling has always worked for leadership.
  3. They're demographic-proof. Everyone regardless of age, race, or gender everyone enjoys listening to stories.
  • Stories are contagious. They can spread like wildfire without any additional effort from the storyteller.
  • They're easier to remember. According to a psychologist by the name of Jerome Bruner, facts told as if part of a story are 20 times more likely to be remembered.
  • Telling stories shows respect for the audience. Stories get your message across without arrogantly telling listeners what to think or do.
